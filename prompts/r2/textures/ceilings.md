# Round II — Ceiling Textures

> **SIZE: 64 × 64 pixels. Square. PNG. No alpha.**
>
> **STYLE: Pixel art. Hard edges. Banded shading. 12–20 colours max.**
>
> **Both axes must tile seamlessly.** Ceilings render at 62% brightness compared to floors.
> See style-guide.md for setting and pixel art rules.

---

## ceiling_beetyard — Beet Yard Roof Underside (R2 Floor 0)

The Beet Yard is a semi-covered loading area — the ceiling is the underside of a
corrugated iron lean-to roof supported by steel I-beams. Base: near-black (#0e0e0c).
Corrugated iron profile running diagonally across the tile (lower-left to upper-right,
at ~20° angle): ridges in very dark grey (#2a2826), valleys darker (#141412). An I-beam
section crosses horizontally at y=20: 8 px tall, very dark (#1e1e1c), with rust streaking
down from the beam flanges. Rain and condensation staining in vertical streaks from the
corrugation ridges. One small hole in the roofing sheet: 4×3 px near-black void (#080808)
with a faint lighter halo suggesting sky seen through.

**Dominant tone:** near-black corrugated iron with beam

## ceiling_washhouse — Wash House Ceiling (R2 Floor 1)

Underside of corrugated iron ceiling with active condensation and water staining. Same
corrugated iron profile as the beet yard but viewed more directly overhead — corrugation
ridges run left-right across the tile. Heavy water staining: the tile is dominated by
dark rust-water streaks (#2a1808, 2 px wide) running vertically from the ridges. Hanging
condensation droplets on the ridges: 1 px bright highlights (#6a5040) at the drip points.
Dark patches of biological growth between ridges (#141e10). One bolt-head visible: 3 px
octagon, dark grey (#282826).

**Dominant tone:** very dark corrugated iron, rust streaks dominant

## ceiling_slicefloor — Slice Floor Ceiling (R2 Floor 2)

Corrugated iron ceiling with fluorescent light fixture frame (the lights have long
since failed). The corrugation runs left-right. Crossing the tile horizontally at y=28:
a rectangular fluorescent fitting frame (2 px border, dark grey #2a2a28) with a cracked
diffuser panel visible inside (pale grey cracks on near-black, #1e1e1c — the tube is
cold and dead). Rust damage around the fitting where water has pooled above. Machine dust
and cobweb: fine horizontal dark lines (1 px, #1a1a18) stretching between corrugation
ridges, suggesting old cobwebs or dust fibres.

**Dominant tone:** near-black corrugated iron with dead light fixture

## ceiling_diffusion — Diffusion Hall Ceiling (R2 Floor 3)

High vaulted ceiling of the diffusion hall — exposed steel trusses over corrugated iron
sheeting. Two truss bottom chords visible: each is 4 px deep, dark grey (#1e2020),
running diagonally from lower-left to upper-right, crossing the tile at y=14 and y=46.
Between the trusses: corrugated iron (#0e1010 valleys, #1a1c1c ridges) stained with
diffusion steam — a warm amber-brown vapour deposit (#2a1c0c) coating everything in a
thin film. The trusses themselves have a pale mineral scale deposit along their upper edge
(#4a4844, 1 px). Very dark overall — the height of the hall swallows the light.

**Dominant tone:** near-black with truss silhouettes

## ceiling_limehouse — Lime House Ceiling (R2 Floor 4)

Lime-crusted barrel vault ceiling — the kiln chambers have brick vault ceilings thickly
coated with lime. Much lighter than other ceilings in this round. Base: pale grey-white
lime (#8a8682). Brick coursing visible through the lime: regular 16×8 px brick shapes in
slightly darker (#706e6a), with thin mortar joints. Heavy lime stalactite formation near
the tile centre: a drip stalactite (4 px wide, 12 px long) of bright white lime (#c8c6c0)
hanging down, with a rounded drip tip. Soot deposit radiating from one corner (dark
grey-black smear, #1a1816) where kiln gas has vented. The vault curve is suggested by a
very subtle value gradient: slightly darker toward the tile edges, lighter at centre.

**Dominant tone:** pale grey-white lime vault (lightest ceiling in the game)

## ceiling_evaporator — Evaporator Gallery Ceiling (R2 Floor 5)

Pipe-lagged steam pipes dominate the ceiling — this is the underside of the evaporator
gallery's main pipe runs. Multiple large pipes run left-right across the tile at different
heights: one 10 px diameter pipe at y=10 (dark grey steel, #1e2024, with a corroded
flange at tile centre); one 6 px diameter pipe at y=40 (slightly lighter, #282c30).
The space between pipes: corrugated iron sheeting darkened by years of steam (#1a1c1c).
Condensation drips: bright 1 px highlights at the lowest point of each pipe (drip
forming, #4a5060). Mineral scale build-up along the upper contact between pipes and
ceiling: pale grey-white crust (#8a8c8e, 2 px thick). A wisps of pale steam residue
suggested by slightly lighter horizontal bands (#202428) between the pipes.

**Dominant tone:** very dark industrial metal with pipe cross-sections

## ceiling_panhouse — Pan House Ceiling (R2 Floor 6)

Heavy timber and iron ceiling — the pan house has large pan vessels that require strong
overhead structure. Two massive timber beams run left-right, each 10 px tall, in very
dark near-black brown (#161412). The steel ceiling plate between beams: dark grey
(#1a1c1c) with a regular rivet pattern (3 px round rivets, dark grey #2a2c2e, at 12 px
intervals both axes). Sugar residue has crystallised on the ceiling: pale off-white
crystal clusters (#b0ae a8) hang from the rivet heads and beam lower edges. One crack in
the ceiling plate: a dark irregular fracture line (#0a0a08, 1-2 px wide) running diagonally.

**Dominant tone:** near-black with beam and rivet structure

## ceiling_sugartower — Sugar Tower Ceiling (R2 Floor 7)

The ceiling of the sugar tower is fully encrusted in crystalline sugar — as pale and
strange as the floor and walls. The same crystal palette as wall_sugar and floor_sugartower:
pale off-white (#c8c6c0), slightly yellow-white (#d0caba), pale grey (#a8a6a0), with
dark gaps between clusters (#282624). Crystal formations hang downward from the ceiling:
stalactite-like growths, 3–6 px wide, 8–16 px long, with angular faceted tips. The
largest formation is near tile centre (10 px wide, 20 px long) with bright-face highlights
(#d8d6d0) on the upward-facing facets. Amber staining concentrated in the lower stalactite
tips (#c8bc90) where dripping molasses has concentrated the colour. One dark circular
void (#0c0c0a, 8 px diameter) — a hatch opening to the roof above.

**Dominant tone:** pale off-white crystalline stalactites
