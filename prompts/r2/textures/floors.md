# Round II — Floor Textures

> **SIZE: 64 × 64 pixels. Square. PNG. No alpha.**
>
> **STYLE: Pixel art. Hard edges. Banded shading. 12–20 colours max.**
>
> **Both axes must tile seamlessly.**
> See style-guide.md for setting and pixel art rules.

---

## floor_beetyard — Beet Yard Ground (R2 Floor 0)

Compacted earth and concrete yard surface, churned by decades of beet deliveries.
Base: dark grey-brown compacted dirt (#1e1c18). Scattered across the tile: small angular
gravel pieces (2–3 px, mid-grey #3a3630), crushed beet fibre stains (dark purple-brown
patches, #2a1818, irregular 4–6 px blobs), and embedded concrete pad sections (flat grey
rectangles, #2e2c28, occupying roughly 1/4 of the tile). One embedded iron rail sleeper:
a 4 px wide dark band running diagonally lower-left to upper-right (#1a1816), with rust
staining along the edges (#3a2010). Standing water in the lowest depression: a 6×4 px
near-black puddle (#0e0e0c) reflecting a faint lighter pixel at centre.

**Dominant tone:** very dark grey-brown earth

## floor_washhouse — Wash House Trough Floor (R2 Floor 1)

Flooded concrete floor with standing water and beet residue. Base: very dark grey
concrete (#1a1a18). The floor is partially covered in dark standing water (#0a0c0e, almost
black) — irregular water pools occupying roughly 40% of the tile, with hard pixel-art
edges (no anti-aliasing). Beet-stained concrete where not flooded: dark reddish-brown
deposit (#2a1812). Algae bloom: a 6×8 px patch of dark green-black (#141e10). Rust from
iron trough supports: scattered dark orange flecks (#3a2010). Water reflection: one 2×2 px
bright highlight in the largest pool (#2a3040, slightly blue-grey). Floor drain grate
visible: a 6×6 px iron grid pattern (#141412) near tile centre.

**Dominant tone:** near-black with dark water pools

## floor_slicefloor — Slice Floor (R2 Floor 2)

Metal grid-plate floor — the cutting machines sit on raised steel grid-plate. The tile
shows the grid-plate pattern: a regular 8×8 px diamond-plate or grid texture in dark grey
steel (#1e2020), with recessed grid openings (near-black, #0e1010) forming a regular
diamond grid. The plate surface has heavy rust: solid rust patches (#3a1e0e) covering
roughly 30% of the plate surface in irregular shapes. Cutting debris: dark brown-red fibre
fragments (1–2 px, #2a1810) caught in the grid openings. The floor is slightly damp:
rust-water drips have left dark trails across the grid surface, running roughly vertically
(following gravity of the tilted plate), each trail 1 px wide in very dark brown.

**Dominant tone:** dark rusted grid-plate steel

## floor_diffusion — Diffusion Hall Floor (R2 Floor 3)

Concrete floor stained by diffusion juice (dark molasses-coloured liquid). Base concrete
(#1a1816). Thick organic staining in dark amber-brown (#2e1c0c) covering most of the tile
surface in irregular puddle shapes with hard pixel edges. Crystallised residue deposits:
small pale patches where the juice has dried and crystallised (#8a7a5a, 2–4 px clusters).
One large irregular pool of near-black liquid (#080806) near tile centre, reflecting a
single pale pixel highlight. Footprint impressions: two partial boot-sole shapes pressed
into the dried staining (#1a1208, 8×5 px each, positioned diagonally across the tile).

**Dominant tone:** very dark amber-brown organic staining

## floor_limehouse — Lime House Floor (R2 Floor 4)

Lime-coated brick floor heavily dusted with white calcium powder. Base: scorched brick
(#2a1e14). Over this: a thick coating of pale grey-white lime dust (#9a9690, slightly
lighter than the wall texture's lime). The dust is not uniform — it has been disturbed by
foot traffic, leaving darker channels (#2a2420) where the brick beneath shows through,
and thick drifts in undisturbed corners (almost pure #c0bcb8). Scattered across the tile:
small pale chips of lime (2–3 px, bright off-white #d0cec8) that have spalled from the
kiln walls. A 6×4 px scorch mark in dark grey-black (#141210) where something hot was
set down. The overall effect is lighter and more ghostly than other floors.

**Dominant tone:** pale grey-white lime dust (lightest floor in the game)

## floor_evaporator — Evaporator Gallery Floor (R2 Floor 5)

Damp metal grating floor with condensation and scale. Diamond-plate steel grating
(#1e2024), same pattern as Slice Floor but cleaner and in a blue-grey steel rather than
rust-red. The dominant feature is condensation: the entire tile has a wet sheen — every
raised plate surface has a bright reflection pixel (1 px, #3a4050) and every groove is
filled with dark condensate water (#0c0e10). Calcium scale deposits: white mineral crust
(#9a9c9e) built up along the grating edges in an irregular band running from lower-left
to upper-right. Light steam residue discolouration: pale warm-grey patches on the plate
surface (#2a2c30) where steam has deposited, contrasting with the cooler dark grid openings.

**Dominant tone:** dark blue-grey metal grating with wet highlights

## floor_panhouse — Pan House Floor (R2 Floor 6)

Heavy concrete floor with vibration cracks and machine grease. Base: very dark grey
concrete (#1a1a18) but with a distinctive coarse aggregate texture — visible aggregate
stones (2–3 px, #3a3830) pressed into the floor surface. Wide crack network: thick dark
cracks (#0a0a08, 2 px wide) radiating from a central stress point in a rough web pattern,
caused by years of centrifuge vibration. Machine grease: near-black oil/grease stains
(#100e0c) in irregular pools, one large (8×10 px) and three small (4×4 px). Sugar residue:
pale yellow-white crystalline crust in corners and along the crack edges (#c0b880, 2–4 px
clusters). One embedded iron drain cover: 8×8 px, circular profile in dark grey (#2a2c2e),
with rust staining around its rim.

**Dominant tone:** very dark grey concrete with cracks

## floor_sugartower — Sugar Tower Floor (R2 Floor 7)

The top floor. Sugar crystal accretion covers the floor as thickly as the walls.
The floor surface is almost entirely crystal — pale off-white (#c8c6c0) with angular
faceted clusters of the same crystal colours as wall_sugar. Between clusters: narrow dark
gaps (#282624) showing the original floor surface. The crystals on the floor are slightly
yellower than the walls — higher molasses content (#d0c890). A few darker amber crystal
zones (#b89840) where the concentration is highest. Near the tile centre: a circular
depression where a drain once was — the crystals have grown around it, leaving a dark
opening (6 px diameter, #0e0e0c) rimmed with the densest crystal formation. The overall
effect is pale and cold — the most unearthly floor in the game.

**Dominant tone:** pale off-white crystalline crust
