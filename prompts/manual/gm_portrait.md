# Games Master dossier portrait — `assets/manual/art/gm_portrait.png`

> **Portrait 4:5 (target 832×1024). PNG.**
>
> **STYLE: aged oil-painting portrait in an ornate gilt frame, painted in a
> grainy late-80s horror-paperback manner. NO text or lettering.**

A formal three-quarter portrait of **The Games Master**, as if commissioned for
a hotel lobby a century ago. He sits in a high-backed crimson chair against a
near-black brown backdrop.

- Black Victorian suit, narrow lapels, black cravat with a tiny gold pin.
- **Red skull mask** (#a01818) instead of a face: smooth lacquered bone planes,
  hollow black eye sockets, a fixed grin of painted teeth. The mask reads as
  lucha-libre-meets-death-mask, elegant rather than gory.
- **Red skeletal gloves** — bone pattern embroidered on black leather — folded
  over the skull pommel of a black cane.
- Lighting: single warm candle-glow from the lower left (#c08030), so the mask
  is lit from below. A barely-visible red rim light traces his right shoulder.
- The ornate gold frame (#c0a060, worn and chipped) is part of the image,
  surrounding the portrait on all four sides. A small empty brass nameplate at
  the bottom of the frame — blank, no text.
- Subtle wrongness: one brushstroke of the background behind his shoulder is
  smeared, as if something moved while the paint was wet.

**Dominant tones:** blacks and dark umber, aged gold frame, the red of the mask
the only saturated colour.
