# Round 1 — Opera House Posters & Paintings (4 decals)

> 48 × 64 px, pixel art, PNG with alpha. See style-guide.md for all hard rules.

Hung throughout the Grand Foyer, Auditorium, Gallery, and Flies. Period:
turn-of-the-century playbills, decades of dust. They should feel like the
building still believes a show is coming.

---

## 1. poster_gala — `assets/decals/poster_gala.png`
Aged cream playbill advertising a professional wrestling event in the year 1900,
not an opera or gala. The main title must read **"CITY OF LIGHTS"** with
**"CHAMPIONSHIP WRESTLING"** directly beneath it. Centre: two turn-of-the-century
catch wrestlers grappling inside a simple roped ring on the Opera House stage,
rendered as large readable silhouettes. Include the date **1900** and use chunky
period text-bars for secondary billing. Deep red title band, foxing, and a curled
corner (use alpha at the torn corner). No opera singers, orchestra, or gala imagery.

## 2. poster_masque — `assets/decals/poster_masque.png`
Midnight-blue playbill: two pale theatrical masks side by side. The comedy
mask's smile is a little too wide and smeared red. Text-bars beneath.

## 3. poster_seance — `assets/decals/poster_seance.png`
Purple-black bill for a spiritualist act: a chalk-pale ring (table circle) with
Morgana Lavey's head inside it, adapted from
`/Volumes/A101/02. Vendetta at the Opera House/VENDETTA YT Builder/TAS-Morgana-Lavey.png`.
Preserve her recognizable long wavy dark-brown hair, dramatic winged eyes, arched
brows, full dark lips, two beauty marks, and drop earrings, but convert the head
to the limited-palette, hard-edged early-90s FPS pixel-art style. Her face should
look like a spectral printed portrait, not a generic ghost. Text-bars below.
Ink-faded, water stain in one corner.

## 4. portrait_gm — `assets/decals/portrait_gm.png`
Gilt-framed oil portrait, edge-to-edge frame (no alpha). Dark canvas; a
gentleman in a top hat, face a pale smudge with two pin-prick RED eyes — the
only saturated red in the image. He is the Games Master, painted long before
anyone now alive worked here. Crackled varnish texture suggested with banding.
